Recent Work

Diffusion-Based 3D Microfluidic Separation Device

Designed and developed a three-dimensional microfluidic lab-on-chip device for particle separation based on laminar flow and diffusion principles. Engineered a 3D channel architecture to enhance separation efficiency while suppressing unwanted convective mixing and maintaining flow stability. Validated experimental results through multiphysics simulations in COMSOL, correlating diffusion-driven particle transport with device geometry to confirm performance and optimize design parameters.

EWOD Continuous 2D Chip (Original Concept)

Conceived and developed a continuous electrowetting-on-dielectric (EWOD) 2D microfluidic platform from initial concept to prototype. Conducted experimental validation and performance characterization, supported by analytical modeling and data evaluation in MATLAB.

Magneto-Rheological Characterization of Cellulose Nanocrystal Suspensions

Executed an experimental study to investigate the rheological and magneto-responsive behavior of cellulose nanocrystal (CNC) suspensions at varying concentration ratios. Integrated a rheometer with a controlled magnetic field system to evaluate field-dependent viscosity and flow characteristics. Analyzed experimental data to assess potential biomedical applications, with a focus on developing injectable fluid systems analogous to synovial fluid.

Calibration and Performance Validation of Precision Syringe Pump System

Designed and executed a controlled validation study to assess the accuracy, repeatability, and flow stability of a newly calibrated syringe pump. Developed experimental protocols to measure volumetric consistency across varying flow rates, quantified deviations from target outputs, and evaluated system reliability under steady-state operating conditions. Analyzed performance data to verify compliance with specified tolerances and provided technical feedback to support product refinement and quality assurance.

Micro-Scale Copper Etching via Controlled Laminar Flow

Designed and executed a microfluidic etching process using laminar flow control to precisely shape micro-scale copper wires within a chip. Engineered a sandwich-flow configuration in which the etchant stream was stabilized between buffer layers, enabling controlled diffusion-driven material removal by tuning relative flow rates. Achieved programmable micro-structuring of copper elements, later utilized as sacrificial molds to fabricate complex, cost-effective microchannel geometries for advanced microfluidic applications.
